Yoga Practices

Sun Salutation (Surya Namaskar)

Sun Salutation is a sequence of 12 powerful yoga poses that provide a full body workout.

Instructions:

  1. Start in Mountain Pose (Tadasana).
  2. Raise your arms upward (Urdhva Hastasana).
  3. Bend forward into Standing Forward Bend (Uttanasana).
  4. Step or jump back to Plank Pose.
  5. Lower into Four-Limbed Staff Pose (Chaturanga Dandasana).
  6. Lift your chest into Upward-Facing Dog (Urdhva Mukha Svanasana).
  7. Push back into Downward-Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana).
  8. Step or jump forward into Standing Forward Bend.
  9. Rise up with arms overhead.
  10. Return to Mountain Pose.
  11. Repeat the sequence for desired repetitions.

Warrior II (Virabhadrasana II)

Warrior II is a standing yoga pose that strengthens the legs, opens the hips, and improves balance.

Instructions:

  1. Start in Mountain Pose.
  2. Step your right foot forward into a wide stance.
  3. Turn your left foot slightly inward and your right foot out 90 degrees.
  4. Bend your right knee over your right ankle, keeping your thigh parallel to the ground.
  5. Extend your arms out to the sides, parallel to the ground.
  6. Gaze over your right hand.
  7. Hold for 3-5 breaths.
  8. Repeat on the other side.

Child's Pose (Balasana)

Child's Pose is a resting yoga pose that stretches the hips, thighs, and ankles while calming the mind.

Instructions:

  1. Kneel on the ground with your knees wide apart.
  2. Sit back onto your heels.
  3. Extend your arms forward and lower your chest toward the ground.
  4. Stretch your arms out in front of you and lower your forehead to the ground.
  5. Hold for 5-10 breaths.

Tree Pose (Vrksasana)

Tree Pose is a balancing yoga pose that strengthens the legs and improves focus and concentration.

Instructions:

  1. Start in Mountain Pose.
  2. Shift your weight onto your left foot.
  3. Place the sole of your right foot on your inner left thigh or calf.
  4. Bring your hands to prayer position at your heart.
  5. Focus on a point in front of you to help with balance.
  6. Hold for 3-5 breaths.
  7. Repeat on the other side.

Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana)

Cobra Pose is a backbend that strengthens the spine, opens the chest, and improves posture.

Instructions:

  1. Start by lying on your stomach with your hands under your shoulders.
  2. Pressinto your hands and lift your chest off the ground, keeping your elbows close to your body.
  3. Engage your back muscles and lift your gaze upward.
  4. Hold for 3-5 breaths.
  5. Lower back down to the ground.

Corpse Pose (Savasana)

Corpse Pose is a resting pose that calms the mind and body.

Instructions:

  1. Find a quiet and comfortable place to lie down.
  2. Extend your legs and arms comfortably to the sides.
  3. Close your eyes and relax your entire body.
  4. Breathe deeply and let go of any tension or stress.
  5. Hold for 5-10 minutes.
