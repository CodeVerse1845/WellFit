Boxing Techniques

Jab Technique

The jab is a quick, straight punch thrown with the lead hand from the guard position.

Instructions:

  1. Start in your boxing stance with your guard up.
  2. Extend your lead arm straight out, rotating your fist as you punch.
  3. Snap your punch back quickly to your guard position.
  4. Keep your rear hand up to protect your face.
  5. Practice the motion slowly at first, then increase speed as you improve.

Hook Technique

The hook is a powerful punch thrown with a bent arm in a semi-circular motion.

Instructions:

  1. Start in your boxing stance with your guard up.
  2. Rotate your lead foot and pivot on your rear foot as you throw the hook.

Uppercut Technique

The uppercut is a powerful punch thrown with a bent arm in a vertical motion.

Instructions:

  1. Start in your boxing stance with your guard up.
  2. Bend your knees slightly and rotate your body as you throw the uppercut.
  3. Keep your elbow close to your body and aim for the opponent's chin.

Defense Techniques

Defense techniques are essential for protecting yourself from your opponent's punches.

Instructions:

  1. Start in your boxing stance with your guard up.
  2. Use head movement to slip, bob, and weave to avoid punches.
  3. Block punches with your arms and gloves.
  4. Practice footwork to maintain distance and angles.

Sparring Techniques

Sparring is a controlled practice fight that allows you to apply your boxing skills in a realistic setting.

Instructions:

  1. Start with a partner and agree on a set of rules and goals.
  2. Practice different combinations and techniques in a controlled manner.
  3. Focus on defense and counter-punching as well as offense.
  4. Communicate with your partner and provide feedback to help each other improve.

Pivot Techniques

The pivot in boxing is a footwork technique used for defense, angle creation, and counterattacks while maintaining balance and mobility.

Instructions:

  1. Stand in your boxing stance with your lead foot slightly pointed forward.
  2. Keep your rear foot light and use the ball of your lead foot as a pivot point.
  3. Rotate your body and rear foot in the desired direction while keeping your balance.
  4. Maintain a strong stance to quickly counter or evade attacks.
  5. Reps: 10-15 pivots per set | Sets: 3-4
